About

Who You Are

I am a PhD student at Rutgers-Newark University in the Department of Physics under the supervision of Prof. Mechele Pavanello. I am interested in the development of new methods for computational chemistry and the application of these methods to the study of chemical reactions and materials. I am currently working in Orbital-Free Density Functional Theory (OF-DFT) and its time-dependent extension (OF-TDDFT). One of my main interests is the develoment of machine learning methods for quantum chemistry.

Research

Current Projects

  • Orbital‑Free Density Functional Theory (OF‑DFT)

    Orbital-Free Density Functional Theory (OF-DFT) is a method that allows for the calculation of the electronic structure of a system without the need to compute individual electronic orbitals. It is a powerful method that can scale linearly with the number of particles in a system, making it ideal for large-scale simulations of materials. The computational cost depends on the approximation used for the kinetic energy density functional (KEDF).

  • Time‑Dependent Orbital‑Free DFT (TD‑OF‑DFT)

    Time-Dependent Orbital-Free Density Functional Theory (TD-OF-DFT) is a theory that allows for the study of systems that evolve in time. Whithin this formalism a system can be study using real-time propagation (RT) or linear-response theory (LR).

  • Machine Learning for Subsystem DFT

    We want to develop machine learning methods for subsystem density functionals (SDFT) that can be used to study the response of a system to an external perturbation.
